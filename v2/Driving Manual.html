2. Train Driving Basics

Typically a train consists of 5 wagons, which can total up to over 167,000 kg in mass. Keep the train length and in mass when driving it. A train possesses considerable inertia even when empty.

The subway driver must pay attention and act according to track signals, track signs and dispatcher instructions to ensure efficient and safe operation of the train.

The trains use rheostat control for starting and braking (Em, Ema508), thyristor control for braking (81-717, Ema509). It takes some time for rheostat to spin into the right position. Try to get a good feeling of this delay.

To avoid damage to the engines and discomfort of the passengers, the current through engines is limited. Effective acceleration and braking of train is 1.5 m/s2.
